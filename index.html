Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before making the switch to HA-based toothpaste.

Is ROZE safe for children? +

Yes. ROZE is specifically formulated to be safe if swallowed — food-grade ingredients only, no fluoride toxicity risk. Many customers introduce it to children as young as 18 months. The Vanilla flavour is wildly popular with kids who refuse mint.

How long until I see results? +

Most customers report reduced sensitivity within 14 days. Visible whitening typically appears between weeks 3 and 6. Enamel remineralization is continuous — the longer you use ROZE, the stronger your teeth become.

Do I need to rinse after brushing? +

No. In fact, do not rinse. Letting the HA stay on your teeth maximizes remineralization. Just spit out the excess. (This is the opposite of fluoride toothpaste — another reason HA is the cleaner choice.)

Does it actually replace fluoride? +

Yes — and arguably better. Fluoride creates a thin protective coating. Hydroxyapatite is the actual mineral your enamel is made of, so it doesn't just coat — it rebuilds. Japan has used HA as a national cavity-prevention standard since 1980.

Can I use it with braces or veneers? +

Yes to both. HA is non-abrasive and biocompatible with all dental work. Many orthodontic patients actively prefer it because it doesn't cause the sensitivity flare-ups that SLS pastes do.
