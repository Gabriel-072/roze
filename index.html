Questions, answered

The honest answers.

Is hydroxyapatite actually safer than fluoride? +

Hydroxyapatite is the natural mineral your teeth and bones are made of — your body produces it. Fluoride is a foreign substance the body cannot use, and at concentrations above 1.0 ppm (most toothpastes) it's regulated as a poison. Japan has used HAP as its national cavity-prevention standard since 1980 with zero reported toxicity cases.

Can my kids use this? +

Yes — Roze is one of the few toothpastes specifically formulated to be safe if swallowed. Many of our customers introduce it to kids as young as 18 months. (The vanilla flavor is wildly popular with children who refuse mint.)

How long until I see results? +

Most customers report reduced sensitivity within 14 days. Visible whitening typically appears between weeks 3 and 6. Enamel rebuilding is a continuous process — the longer you use it, the stronger your teeth become.

Will it work if my tap water has fluoride? +

Yes. Hydroxyapatite works independently and rebuilds enamel regardless of what's in your water. Good news for UAE residents: Dubai and Abu Dhabi tap water is not artificially fluoridated.

Why does it tingle a little at first? +

It's a sign the active ingredients are rebalancing your oral microbiome — usually after years of disruption from SLS. The sensation fades within 1-2 weeks as your mouth chemistry stabilizes.
